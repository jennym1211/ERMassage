FAQ

Q: What is deep tissue?

A: Deep tissue is not as bad as it sounds! It is a technique, not a depth of pressure. It may hurt a little bit, but you shouldn't be in more pain than when you came in. It gets to the deeper muscles that are causing more of the issues to solve them.

Q: What can you do in a 60 min session?

A: This depends on the type of session you're looking for. Most of my clients have a lot of built up pain and tension, so their hour is for solving the issues and pain. If you're looking for relaxation and nothing but relaxation, I could easily do a fullbody massage.

Q:How many sessions will I need to solve an issue?

A: It depends on the issue and depends on how longs it's been bothering you. Most of my clients will start to feel a little better after the first session, but the pain will come back. Massage is not a one and done fix all. It takes repetition to start seeing results because of muscle memory.

Q:What's the difference between massage therapy and chiropractics?

A: Massage therapy works on the muscles and chiropractic care focus on the skeletal structure. They actually go hand in hand if you're looking for more results!

Q: When is the best time to get a massage?

A: Ideally, getting a massage is the best at the end of the day after every activity you may have. I know that's not possible all the time, but even breaks you have in your day are perfect for them. All I ask is please don't go workout after getting the lactic acid moved out of your system.
